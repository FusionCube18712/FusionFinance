Submission thesis

ML proposes. Agents investigate. The verifier challenges. The risk engine decides.

01 / 10
The problem

Autonomous trading is split between two incomplete systems

Pure ML is disciplined but context-blind

Structured models rank opportunities consistently, but do not understand a guidance change, supply shock, or accounting caveat.

Pure LLM is contextual but poorly calibrated

Agents synthesize narrative, but can hallucinate evidence, overstate confidence, and translate prose into unstable position sizes.

Neither system should control a portfolio without the other system checking its blind spots.

02 / 10
The experiment

Three arms belong under one contract; today’s replay does not yet meet it

Pure ML

Structured data only. No agent interpretation.

Pure LLM

Qualitative interpretation only. No proprietary ML proposal.

FusionFinance

ML proposal, investigation, evidence challenge, and risk sizing.

  • Controlled destination: same universe, capital, timestamps, costs, slippage, leverage, limits, kernel, and rebalance cadence.
  • Current legacy replay: same window and timestamps, but different legacy kernels and no shared cost model.
  • The next shared-kernel run is the falsifiable comparison; the current snapshot demonstrates the product and metric path.

06 / 10
Why the design is different

Fusion is a sequence of challenges, not an average of opinions

ML proposes

Expected return, uncertainty, volatility, and liquidity define the candidate set.

Agents investigate

Market, news, fundamentals, and risk roles search for context and falsifiers in parallel.

The system decides

Evidence can reject. The independent market head can veto. Portfolio risk controls size what survives.

The LLM never gets a direct path to execution, and the model never gets to ignore contradictory context.

08 / 10
Practical value and credibility

An auditable decision system matters even when a trade is rejected

  • Quantitative research and replay.
  • Paper-trading decision support.
  • Portfolio monitoring and risk review.
  • Human-in-the-loop institutional workflows.
  • Point-in-time filing and market features.
  • Hash-bound evidence and GPU receipts.
  • Explicit abstain and research-only states.
  • Documented leakage, survivorship, cost, and sample limitations.
Research software, not a profit promiseBacktests can overfit. Historical LLM analysis can leak through pretraining. Paper fills differ from live execution. FusionFinance makes those constraints visible and machine-checkable.

The conclusion
Not the agent.
Not the model.
The auditable fusion.

Autonomous finance should not be controlled by an unverified language model or an isolated statistical model. FusionFinance combines machine learning, agentic reasoning, quantitative verification, and risk control into one auditable decision system.
