# FusionFinance demo video script

Target length: 4:10. Master format: 1920×1080 at 30 fps.

The narration and timing below are the public source of truth for the final edit. Visual direction, caption beats, and machine-readable timing live in [`video-storyboard.json`](video-storyboard.json).

## 0:00 — Hook

Should a statistical model or an AI agent control an autonomous portfolio? One finds patterns; the other reads context. FusionFinance gives each one job, then requires evidence before risk can authorize a position.

## 0:14 — The controlled destination

The three-system experiment is the destination, not the claim we already have. Its frozen contract puts Pure ML, Pure LLM, and FusionFinance on the same universe, clock, capital, costs, slippage, limits, and rebalance schedule. Today’s public artifact is an older retrospective replay. It shares the window and timestamps, but uses different legacy kernels and no common cost model. We show it as a product and metric demonstration while the controlled run remains the next falsifiable test.

## 0:44 — Pure ML

Pure ML consumes structured, point-in-time market and filing features. It ranks opportunities quickly and applies the same numerical rule every time. In this legacy snapshot, its curve returned plus 1.7 percent with a 0.76 Sharpe ratio and a minus 2.9 percent maximum drawdown across 109 stored account values. But the model cannot read why guidance changed, challenge a management narrative, or recognize an unsupported citation. Trade count is a dash because the historical ledger was not retained; the dash does not mean zero trades.

## 1:15 — Pure LLM

Pure LLM begins with qualitative market interpretation and no proprietary ML proposal. It can connect news, fundamentals, market structure, and risk into a readable thesis. That is valuable, but prose is not calibration. The stored arm is a retrospective batch, not a live multi-agent run. In this provisional legacy snapshot, it returned minus 10.3 percent, with a minus 0.90 Sharpe and a minus 23.8 percent drawdown. Rich explanation did not become disciplined portfolio construction.

## 1:46 — One FusionFinance decision

FusionFinance is not a vote and it is not an average. The modern contract requires a candidate to carry a ticker, action, horizon, expected outcome, evidence references, provenance, and timestamp before verification and risk. Now inspect the actual legacy Micron record. It proposed a sell with 60 percent confidence and the rationale, parabolic three-month surge with volume blow-off. It did not record an expected move, horizon, sources, or prospective seal. Projected into the modern gate, the record fails the contract. The replay keeps the final action rejected and the position at zero. This is not proof that the full pipeline executed historically. It is an auditable demonstration that missing fields stay visible and an incomplete candidate cannot advance to capital. In the next controlled run, agents investigate, evidence challenges, the market verifier checks direction, and risk sizes only contract-complete candidates.

## 2:36 — Results and claim boundary

Read this result as a legacy snapshot, not a fair win. The stored Fusion line shows plus 10.1 percent return, a 2.05 Sharpe, and a minus 3.9 percent maximum drawdown, versus plus 8.7 percent for SPY. But the Fusion curve has sparse five-session marks: only 21 nonzero changes across 109 stored account values, while the comparison arms are marked more frequently. That cadence can hide intra-interval troughs and weakens drawdown comparability. The legacy Fusion curve also contains the ML champion, and every historical LLM thesis fails today’s evidence contract. These numbers are provisional, not causal proof of outperformance. They demonstrate the interface, metric pipeline, and claim boundary; the next shared-kernel run must earn the comparison.

## 3:26 — AMD compute receipts

The AMD evidence is a separate recorded workload, not the source of these legacy curves. It shows 72 expanding walk-forward ranker retrains in PyTorch on ROCm and HIP, architecture gfx1100, 51.5 gigabytes of reported VRAM, and 231.29 seconds. Cross-checked SHA-256 receipts protect integrity; they are not independent attestation, and utilization is unavailable.

## 3:55 — Close

FusionFinance gives each system one job: ML proposes, agents investigate, evidence challenges, and risk decides. It is an auditable research system—not financial advice and never a profit promise.
